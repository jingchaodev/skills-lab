# Researching AI Startups

## Core rule

Build an evidence-backed understanding of the company. **Do not decide whether the user should join it.**

Use plain, concrete language. Explain the user, problem, product, constraints, and causal mechanism before repeating company positioning.

## Choose a mode

| Request | Mode | Required reference |
|---|---|---|
| Category, market, theme, or list of companies | Company selection | `references/company-selection.md` |
| One named company | Single-company research | `references/single-company-research.md` |

Always apply `references/source-quality.md`. For interviews, podcasts, talks, YouTube videos, or transcripts, also apply `references/interview-notes.md`. Before writing files, apply `references/output-formats.md` and honor the project’s requested output location and format. If the user gives no destination, use a relative placeholder such as `<company>/`; never infer a private path from memory, profile context, or the current environment.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the research question, scope, requested output, and existing files.
2. Choose **Company selection** or **Single-company research**.
3. Build a coverage map before searching.
4. Search primary sources first and look deliberately for contradictory evidence.
5. Open or retrieve complete sources before interpreting them.
6. Record source metadata and support every important claim.
7. Mark unknowns and keep discovered sources, saved transcripts, and completed notes as separate states.
8. Validate the output against the requested format and source rules.

## Stop conditions

Stop when the requested scope is covered, important claims have traceable sources, contradictions and unknowns are visible, and another search round produces little new evidence. Do not hide gaps to make the research look complete.

## Do not

- Do not issue `Join`, `Watch`, or `Pass` verdicts.
- Do not rank company quality by funding or media attention.
- Do not treat founder or company statements as independent evidence.
- Do not treat a customer logo as verified production use.
- Do not count a discovered source or saved transcript as completed research.
- Do not download audio or video unless the user asks.
- Do not write from search snippets when the full source can be opened.
- Do not invent missing founders, customers, metrics, dates, pricing, or technical details.
